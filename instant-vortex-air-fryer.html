Why it made the list

If you've been on the fence about air fryers, this is the one that settles it. The Instant Vortex hits the sweet spot between capacity, features and price — you get a proper family-sized 5.7L drawer, six cooking programmes, and the ClearCook window that lets you check your food without opening the drawer and losing the heat.

It cooks fast, it's cheaper to run than your oven, and the touch-button interface is simple enough that you don't need the manual after the first use. For £40 it's honestly hard to find a reason not to own one.

Who it's for

Anyone cooking for a household of two to four who wants to spend less time and energy in the kitchen. It's ideal if you're constantly firing up the oven just to cook one thing, or if you want crispier results with less oil and less washing up. The ClearCook window is genuinely useful once you start using it — being able to check on food without opening the drawer is a small thing that makes a big difference.
