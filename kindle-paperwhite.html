Kindle Paperwhite

Waterproof, glare-free, and lighter than a paperback. The best way to carry a thousand books.

Why it made the list

The Kindle Paperwhite sits in a sweet spot that very few products manage to hit — it does one thing exceptionally well, at a price that doesn't require much deliberation. If you read even a handful of books a year, this pays for itself quickly in convenience alone.

The 6.8-inch glare-free display is genuinely comfortable to read on for hours, indoors or out. The adjustable warm light means late-night reading without straining your eyes, and at 205g it's lighter than most paperbacks.

Key specs

Display6.8" glare-free, 300 ppi
Storage16 GB
BatteryUp to 10 weeks
WaterproofIPX8 — up to 2m for 60 minutes
Weight205g
ConnectivityWi-Fi, USB-C

What we liked and didn't

Pros

  • Battery lasts weeks, not hours
  • Genuinely waterproof — pool and bath safe
  • Warm light makes night reading easy
  • USB-C charging (finally)
  • Lighter than most paperbacks

Cons

  • Locked into the Kindle ecosystem
  • No audiobook speaker — needs Bluetooth
  • Lock screen ads on the base model

Who it's for

Anyone who reads regularly and wants a dedicated device that removes the distractions of a phone or tablet. It's particularly good for commuters, travellers, and anyone who reads before bed — the warm light genuinely makes a difference compared to a backlit screen.

The verdict

At £95 it's comfortably the best e-reader you can buy under £100. The build quality, display, and battery life punch well above the price. If you've been on the fence, this is the one to go for.
